Key Concepts

Orch OR (Objective Reduction)

Penrose-Hameroff theory: quantum states in microtubules collapse by gravitational self-energy, producing conscious moments — here repurposed as routing primitives.

BLAP (Bio-Layered Address Protocol)

64-bit addresses assigned to each node in the mycelial mesh, replacing MAC addresses with biologically-inspired identifiers for self-organizing topologies.

QSP (Quantum Sync Protocol)

Three-phase handshake: RESONATE → PHASE-SYNC → ENTANGLE. Establishes entanglement pairs for zero-latency data transfer across the bio-quantum network.

Entanglement Swapping

Extends quantum channels: if A↔B and B↔C are entangled, a Bell measurement at B creates a direct A↔C link — enabling long-range non-local communication.

Decoherence Storm

Environmental noise causes rapid quantum state collapse, severing mycelial links. The mesh self-heals by regrowing links at higher resonance cost.

PPP (Penrose Privacy Protocol)

HITL ethical filter: packets are scored by entropy delta (ΔS). Positive entropy → approved. Negative entropy (causality violation) → dropped.
